Origins

"From the beginning Spartans were a weapon of last resort. We were built for combat and raised for war." — Master Chief Petty Officer John-117

ORION Project

The genesis of the SPARTAN programs occurred in 2321 with the activation of the ORION Project. Launched by the Office of Naval Intelligence, the project was named after the Greek hero Orion. The aim of the ORION Project was to create a special forces unit of biochemically augmented supersoldiers. Under the operational control of Naval Special Warfare Group One, the project was created to combat the growing Insurrection throughout the human Outer Colonies. The project enjoyed limited success but was ultimately disbanded after several years; however, its legacy lived on in the form of the subsequent SPARTAN programs. After the establishment of the SPARTAN-II program years later, the ORION Project was retroactively dubbed SPARTAN-I program. These candidates underwent rigorous testing and augmentations throughout the project's duration. However, the outcomes did not meet expectations, and the candidates were eventually integrated back into the regular military structure after the project was deemed ineffective and subsequently terminated. The lessons learned from ORION laid the groundwork for future programs aimed at developing enhanced soldiers for the UNSC.

the Spartan-IIs

Much about the SPARTAN-II program remains classified and cannot be disclosed. However, ONI has permitted us to share the following information: lead by the late Dr. Catherine Halsey The SPARTAN-II program marked the first significant success in the SPARTAN initiative, aimed at creating elite soldiers through mechanical and biological augmentation. Originally conceived as the second generation of the ORION Project, it was referred to as "Generation-II ORION." The SPARTAN-II program was the first to combine advanced exoskeleton technology with the soldiers' enhanced physiques. SPARTAN-IIs underwent the most extensive and invasive physical augmentations of all the Spartan generations, beginning the process at the early military age of 18 to 20. This method increased their physical mass and height to approximately seven feet. Generation II of the ORION Project was initiated in 2526 when it became necessary to explore more effective methods for combating the Covenant.

Augmentation

  • Occipital Capillary Reversal:

    Surgery is performed on the occipital lobe of the left cerebral hemisphere, the visual processing center of the brain. The surgeon reverses the direction of blood flow in the capillaries to boost blood flow beneath the retina's rods and cones, enhancing visual perception. However, 11% of subjects may face retinal rejection and detachment, potentially causing permanent blindness.

  • Carbide Ceramic Ossification:

    The candidate's bones are exposed via surgery, and advanced carbide ceramic material is grafted onto the skeletal structure. This makes the bones virtually unbreakable, but the coverage does not exceed 3% of total bone mass due to white blood cell necrosis. There is a 3.8% failure rate due to potential mutations and compromised bone marrow integrity,.

  • Catalytic Thyroid Implant:

    A platinum pellet is implanted in the left thyroid gland, releasing human growth hormone to boost skeletal and muscle tissue growth. This hormone targets muscle fibers and osteoblasts for significant gains in muscle mass and bone strength. However, 2% of subjects may develop elephantiasis and experience suppressed sexual drive.

  • Muscular Enhancement Injections:

    A protein complex is injected intramuscularly to increase muscle strength and endurance by enhancing the muscles' intracellular machinery. This results in increased density of connective tissues and decreased recovery time. 5% of test subjects may experience a fatal cardiac volume increase.

  • Superconducting Fibrification of Neural Dendrites:

    This alteration enhances bioelectrical nerve transduction, resulting in a 300% increase in reflexes, along with anecdotal evidence of improved intelligence, memory, and creativity. However, 12% of subjects may contract Parkinson's Disease or Fletcher's Syndrome.

MJOLNIR

The MJOLNIR Powered Assault Armor System,(pronunciation:ˈmjɔːlnɪər, myol-neer) is a technologically advanced combat exoskeleton system designed to vastly improve the strength, speed, agility, reflexes, and durability of a Spartan supersoldier. Though the development program is rendered in all capital letters (i.e. Project: MJOLNIR), the armor itself is called Mjolnir.The suit is named after the hammer of Thor in Norse mythology.

The Mjolnir armor, developed alongside the SPARTAN-II program by ONI's Materials Group, is the most advanced tactical military hardware available. Designed by Dr. Catherine Halsey, Mjolnir aims to enhance the physical capabilities of SPARTAN soldiers through mechanical and biological augmentation. Each suit is incredibly costly, comparable to a small starship. Project Mjolnir spans over three decades, evolving from a simple suit of armor into a pioneering effort to break technological barriers. The armor enhances physical performance and integrates an artificial intelligence network, marking a significant advancement over earlier powered exoskeletons. Mjolnir suits feature multilayered construction, with a strong alloy shell augmented to deflect some energy weapon strikes. The inner gel layer regulates temperature and adjusts its density, while the entire suit is a sealed system for operations in hazardous environments. It is also resistant to EMP and radiation, with effective filters for toxins. A notable feature of the Mjolnir armor is the polymerized lithium niobocene layer, which amplifies force and reaction time. This system is so responsive that it can only be operated by enhanced humans, as ordinary individuals risk injury. The armor includes a super-dense optical computer memory that supports sophisticated artificial intelligences, allowing for seamless integration with the user’s neural interface. This neural link enables direct control over the suit’s movements, creating a symbiotic relationship between soldier and armor. Mjolnir also offers numerous additional functionalities, such as attachment points for weapons, an advanced heads-up display (HUD) for vital data, and health monitoring systems. Weighing approximately 1,000 pounds, the final Mjolnir battlesuit represents the pinnacle of military technology, designed to be an unparalleled asset in combat.

Inner Components

  • Inner Skinsuit: Made of moisture-absorbing synthetic material linked to an environmental control computer and neural interface, regulating temperature and fit.
  • Hydrostatic Gel: A gel layer that regulates temperature and conforms to the wearer's shape, also pressurizable for high G-force protection.
  • Liquid Metal Crystal Piezoelectric Layer: Enhances mobility and strength, reacting to electrical charges to improve physical performance.
  • Knitted-Crystal Computer Memory Layer: Supports AI integration and constitutes a significant portion of the suit's cost.
  • Pressure Seal: Ensures airtight integrity for underwater or space operations, only failing under extreme conditions.
  • Titanium Nanocomposite Bodysuit: Provides strength and flexibility, serving as additional protection against ballistic attacks.
  • Biofoam Injectors: Automatically fill and seal wounds, offering temporary protection until medical attention can be obtained.
  • Reactive Circuits: Amplify reaction time by connecting directly to the user's neural interface.
  • Lockdown System: Protects muscles and joints from injuries by rigidifying the suit in high-impact situations.

Outer Components

  • Integrated Communications Helmet: Features a heads-up display, environmental controls, and a direct neural interface for seamless operation.
  • Titanium Alloy Outer Shell: Provides resilient protection against small arms fire and is augmented to dissipate heat from energy weapons.
  • Memory Processor Superconductor Layer: Enables starship-grade AI integration for operational support in the field.
  • Magnetic Weapon Holder Strips: Small magnets on the suit for holding equipment and allowing attachment to metal surfaces in zero gravity.
  • Energy Shield System: Based on reverse-engineered Covenant technology, providing enhanced protection in combat.
  • Power Plant: A microfusion cell supplying power to the suit, replacing earlier fission reactors for safety and efficiency.
  • Power Supply Control Unit: Manages the power distribution within the armor.
  • Fail-Safe Detonation System: A self-destruct mechanism to prevent enemy acquisition of the armor, activated by a specific code.

Organization

The majority of Spartan-IIs serve under Naval Special Warfare, a division of the UNSC Special Forces closely tied to Section Three of the Office of Naval Intelligence, which initiated the SPARTAN-II program. This structure remained intact even after the formation of the new Spartan Operations branch post-war. All Spartan-IIs graduated as petty officers second class, with many advancing in rank; for instance, John-117 became a Master Chief Petty Officer (in fact some say he embodies the rank to the point all MCPOs are called top chief out of respect for the chief) in 2526, and Frederic-104 was promoted to Lieutenant, Junior Grade in 2552. Spartan-IIs operate with considerable autonomy, typically in teams of three to five, although team size and composition vary based on mission requirements. Team leaders select Spartans suited to specific tasks, leading to established arrangements over time due to the dispersal of the original group during the Covenant War. Teams are designated by color for missions, with Blue, Red, and Green teams being the most prominent. Notably, Red Team has sometimes formed smaller splinter units for specific objectives, while Black and Gray teams have maintained their compositions throughout the war.

the Legend of the Master Chief

Master Chief Petty Officer John-117, also known simply by his rank as the "Master Chief" or (as is common in military culture) simply as the "Chief", is a Spartan-II supersoldier of the UNSC Navy's Special Weapons division. He served as one of the most important figures of the Human-Covenant War. After over thirty years of active duty, he is one of the most decorated war veterans of the United Nations Space Command.

Service record

By August of 2552, John had completed over two hundred military operations, including one hundred and thirty-six full campaigns; more than any other UNSC personnel on record at the time. He was one of only Two Spartans designated as Hyper lethal. Though John has commanded nearly every Spartan-II at one time or another, most of his career has been spent as fireteam leader of Blue Team. His actions during the Human-Covenant War earned him every major UNSC service medal and combat award, save for the Prisoner of War Medallion. As the war neared its end, most of John's fellow SPARTAN-IIs were killed in action during the Fall of Reach. Fall of Reach

The Covenant caught Reach completely off guard, launching a surprise attack that decimated defenses and shattered the planet's sense of security. The destruction of the Visigrad relay triggered the Winter Contingency, a plan designed to safeguard humanity in the event of an overwhelming threat. Unfortunately, the alert took weeks to reach the entire planet, leaving many unprepared for the impending assault. In this desperate hour, the Spartans emerged as humanity's last line of defense. They fought valiantly against the overwhelming Covenant forces, exhibiting unparalleled bravery and skill in the face of insurmountable odds. Their efforts were heroic, and many Spartans made the ultimate sacrifice, giving their lives to protect Reach and its inhabitants. It was their courage and determination that provided a glimmer of hope in an otherwise bleak situation, demonstrating the true spirit of the SPARTANs as defenders of humanity. As the Covenant fleet launches a massive attack on Reach, John and the Spartans are assigned to defend vital power generators essential for the planet's defense. In a race against time, John teams up with Spartans Linda-058 and James-005, working tirelessly to prevent the Covenant from accessing crucial navigation data that could spell doom for humanity. Despite their best efforts, the Covenant overwhelms Reach's defenses, resulting in devastating losses for the UNSC. During the chaos, James is tragically killed in action, and Linda suffers severe injuries, ultimately being placed in cryostasis in the hope of future revival. John-117's experiences during the Fall of Reach underscore the immense challenges faced by the SPARTAN-IIs as they engage in a desperate fight against seemingly insurmountable odds. The emotional toll of losing comrades and the relentless threat of the Covenant profoundly shape his journey as humanity's protector, driving him to confront the darkness ahead with unwavering resolve.

Return to Earth

After the fall of Reach, the Master Chief and his AI companion, Cortana, manage to escape the devastation aboard the Pillar of Autumn. In a strategic move, they detonate the ship's engines, resulting in the destruction of the surrounding Covenant fleet. Adrift for a week, they eventually encounter other survivors, including Sergeant Johnson and Corporal Locklear. Together, they seize control of the Covenant flagship, Ascendant Justice, to return to the Reach system. Upon their arrival, they discover remaining Spartans and Vice Admiral Whitcomb. United in purpose, they formulate a plan to thwart the Covenant's impending invasion of Earth. The Chief and his team undertake a daring rescue of trapped Spartans, preparing for the looming threat. However, the Covenant soon discover their location, leading to a fierce battle. In a desperate bid for survival, the UNSC allies with hidden human separatists, but they are ultimately forced to flee. Determined to disrupt the Covenant invasion fleet, the Spartans infiltrate a Covenant space station, setting it to self-destruct before making their escape. The surviving Spartans, along with Sergeant Johnson and Cortana, take the partially repaired UNSC Gettysburg back to Earth to warn of the approaching Covenant forces. Within military circles, he is commonly referred to as "SPARTAN-117" or by the phonetic callsign "Sierra 117." Like all Spartans, John is viewed by the Covenant as a "Demon." However, he is particularly known as "the Demon," a title he earned following the destruction of a significant "alien structure."
