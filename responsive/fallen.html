To those that ventured into the howling dark never to return

This page is dedicated to those who made the ultimate sacrifice in defense of humanity. Their bravery and selflessness in the face of overwhelming odds are a testament to the indomitable spirit of the human race. Many of these names are etched into the memorial at Voi, serving as a poignant reminder of their valor and heroism. Regrettably, not all who sacrificed themselves in the name of humanity are represented here, yet their contributions remain. Among those honored are the heroes whose deeds earned them the Colonial Cross, and other medels of prestigious recognition awarded for extraordinary acts of courage and heroism. Each name tells a story of sacrifice, resilience, and commitment to a cause greater than oneself. As we reflect on their legacy, let us remember the families and friends they left behind and the profound impact of their service. Their dedication not only protected our future but also inspired countless others to carry the torch of hope and perseverance. We honor their memory, cherishing their sacrifices, and strive to uphold the values they embodied in our ongoing fight for survival.

SN:01928-19912-JK Captain Jacob Keys

Jacob Keyes was a commanding officer and one of the most brilliant tacticians in the UNSC Navy, commanding great respect as a strategist and inspirational leader. With thirty-five years of combat experience against both the Covenant and Insurrectionists, he was an almost natural choice to command the UNSC Pillar of Autumn and its secret cargo. Renowned as a war hero, Keyes developed the Keyes Loop tactic, showcasing his exceptional skill and foresight. Born on Earth, Keyes had a childhood marked by time spent around the Pacific Ocean. He had a sister who later resided on the colony world of Dwarka. During his training in Officer Candidate School, he endured a traumatic incident that resulted in the death of fourteen ensigns due to an instructor's error. Keyes suffered plasma burns during this test and chose to protect his instructor rather than testify against him, earning him the attention of Dr. Catherine Halsey. In 2517, shortly after entering service, Keyes was chosen to assist Dr. Halsey with her SPARTAN-II project, tasked with finding suitable subjects for the program. His ability to keep a secret made him a valuable asset to Halsey, and he never forgot the names of the children he observed during this pivotal assignment. Keyes's journey continued as he became a widely known hero during the early Human-Covenant War, leading security troops against a Covenant ambush and sustaining injuries that temporarily sidelined him. Despite being labeled "Schoolmaster" by detractors, he later took in his daughter, Miranda, fostering a close relationship that would shape her future in the military. His military career flourished as he took command of the UNSC Iroquois and later the Pillar of Autumn. Keyes displayed remarkable tactical ingenuity, notably during the Battle of Sigma Octanus IV, where he executed the "Keyes Loop" maneuver, destroying multiple Covenant vessels. This victory earned him a promotion to Captain and recognition within the UNSC. As the Covenant's relentless assault on Reach unfolded, Keyes was given a critical mission: to transport SPARTAN-II commandos deep into Covenant space to capture a High Prophet. However, when the Covenant attacked, he adjusted his plans, offloading most of the Spartans to aid in the defense of the planet. Tragically, Keyes's fate took a dire turn. After bravely attempting to hold off the Covenant forces, he was ultimately forced to retreat aboard the Pillar of Autumn, which was relentlessly pursued and ultimately destroyed by the Covenant. Many believe he went down with the ship, a heroic end befitting a legendary figure. Yet, if you seek the truth behind his final moments, perhaps the Master Chief himself could recount the tale—if you have the courage to ask.

SN:03956-26127-PC Admiral Preston j. Cole

Preston J. Cole is best known for commanding the majority of the UNSC Navy and for his pivotal role in creating the Cole Protocol—a crucial law designed to protect Earth’s location from the Covenant. A military genius, he defeated the Covenant in every major battle he fought, earning accolades that placed his battle record on par with, or even superior to, that of the Spartan-IIs. Tragically, in 2543 during the Battle of Psi Serpentis, Cole was reported killed when a gas giant was detonated, annihilating a Covenant fleet. However, speculation persists among some officers that he may have survived, choosing to retire away from the UNSC to evade the burdens of command. Born on November 3, 2470, in Mark Twain, Missouri, Cole grew up in a large family and faced financial struggles. Despite his humble beginnings, he displayed remarkable intelligence from a young age. At just fourteen, he penned a report on colonial expansion that foreshadowed the Insurrection. Cole enlisted in the UNSC Navy after high school, viewing it as his only opportunity for exploration and adventure among the stars. Cole's early naval career was marked by a series of significant engagements, most notably during the Callisto Incident, where he showcased his tactical acumen. His quick thinking and bold maneuvers earned him rapid promotions, leading to command of a destroyer and later the heavy destroyer UNSC Gorgon. His reputation as a hero grew during the Insurrection, although it came at a personal cost—his marriage to Inna Volkov ended in divorce. As the Covenant threat escalated, Cole re-emerged as a formidable leader. He was appointed to command the UNSC's largest assembled fleet at the time, Battle Group X-Ray, and played a crucial role in the successful counteroffensive against the Covenant at Harvest.

The Cole Protocol

In response to the Covenant's relentless advances, Cole authored the Cole Protocol, which mandated that no UNSC vessel should provide information regarding human locations to the enemy. This protocol became a cornerstone of UNSC strategy, emphasizing the need for secrecy to protect humanity’s future. The Battle of Psi Serpentis and Speculation on Survival During the climactic Battle of Psi Serpentis, Cole's strategic genius was on full display as he led a fleet of 162 warships against overwhelming Covenant forces. Despite the odds, he executed a bold maneuver that resulted in the destruction of numerous enemy vessels. However, in the ensuing chaos, Cole’s flagship, the UNSC Everest, was lost in a spectacular explosion that destroyed the gas giant Viperidae and the remaining Covenant fleet. Following his presumed death, a day of mourning was declared for Cole, hailed as one of humanity's greatest heroes. Yet, speculation surrounding his fate has persisted. Analysis of battle footage led to the belief that Cole might have survived the explosion through a calculated escape using a decoy and slipspace jump. Some theorize that he sought a peaceful life, perhaps reuniting with a former love, Lyrenne Castilla, away from the chaos of war. Vice Admiral Preston J. Cole remains a complex figure in UNSC history—celebrated for his tactical brilliance, his dedication to humanity, and the enduring mystery of his fate. Whether he truly perished at Psi Serpentis or crafted an elaborate escape, Cole's legacy as a military tactician and symbol of hope endures, continuing to inspire those who serve in defense of humanity. For further insights into his life, consider reading "The Impossible Life and Possible Death of Preston J. Cole."

Civilian Consultant 409871-CH DR.Catherine Elizabeth Halsey

Catherine Elizabeth Halsey was born on March 19, 2492, in the coastal city of Port Vernon on the colony of Endymion. Even from a young age, she demonstrated extraordinary intellect and a keen interest in technology and artificial intelligence. Her father nurtured her imagination with adventurous tales, and by adolescence, she was surpassing her parents in knowledge, eager to share her insights. Halsey's early education at the prestigious Endymion Gifted Academy further showcased her prodigious abilities, culminating in a scholarship to Koletre-Browning University, where she completed her second doctoral thesis by the age of fifteen. Her academic journey was marked by a pivotal encounter with Dr. Elias Carver, during which she challenged his outdated algorithm and subsequently caught the attention of Captain Michael Stanforth from ONI. Recognizing her potential, ONI recruited her in 2508, and she soon became a civilian consultant on various classified projects, including the foundational work that would lead to the creation of the SPARTAN-II program. Despite her non-military status, Dr. Halsey played a crucial role in numerous UNSC victories throughout her career, utilizing her expertise in artificial intelligence and military strategy. She was known for her unyielding commitment to her research and her belief that the ends justified the means, a philosophy that would guide her decisions in the years to come. Halsey's contributions to the UNSC were not just limited to the SPARTAN program; her work in developing advanced technologies and strategies had a lasting impact on the military's capabilities. She received the highest honors a civilian could achieve for her efforts, a testament to her influence and dedication. Tragically, Dr. Halsey's life came to an end during the fall of Reach in 2552, where she officially died. However, her legacy continues to resonate within the UNSC, as she remains a symbol of innovation and determination in humanity's fight for survival against overwhelming odds.

SN 48789-20114-AJ Sergeant Major Avery Junior Johnson

"When I joined the Corps, we didn't have any fancy-shmancy tanks. We had sticks! Two sticks, and a rock for the whole platoon - and we had to share the rock! — Sergeant Johnson Sergeant Major Avery Junior Johnson was a renowned senior non-commissioned officer in the UNSC Marine Corps, whose military career spanned crucial conflicts against both the Insurrection and the Covenant. Born on November 23, 2474, in the Greater Chicago Industrial Zone on Earth, Johnson faced a turbulent childhood. After his parents' tumultuous marriage ended, he was raised by his aunt, Marcille, who instilled in him strong values and a deep sense of justice. This upbringing played a significant role in shaping his motivations to join the military, driven by a desire to protect innocents from the threats posed by Insurrectionists. Avery enlisted in the UNSC Marine Corps at the age of nineteen, quickly distinguishing himself through exceptional training and dedication. His skills earned him a place in the highly classified ORION Project, where he underwent bioengineering protocols designed to create super-soldiers. Despite the project's eventual shutdown, Johnson's talents were leveraged in numerous high-stakes operations against Insurrectionists, demonstrating his capability in both sniping and close-quarters combat. During the Human-Covenant War, Johnson became a pivotal figure. He actively resisted the Covenant's initial attack on Harvest and participated in key battles throughout the conflict, including the Fall of Reach and engagements on various installations, His strategic acumen and charismatic leadership were vital in forming an alliance with The Elite Fleet of Retribution after the Great Schism in November 2552. Despite his many accomplishments, Johnson's life met a tragic end when he was killed in the final battle of the war. His legacy endures as a testament to bravery and sacrifice in humanity's struggle for survival against overwhelming odds. Not only was Johnson remembered as a formidable warrior, but he also served as an inspiration to countless soldiers who followed in his footsteps. Avery Johnson's contributions to the UNSC and humanity's struggle against the Covenant have been memorialized in various ways. On March 3, 2553, a portrait of Johnson was displayed among the fallen heroes at the Voi Memorial. The Avery J. Johnson Academy of Military Science was named in his honor, serving as a training ground for the SPARTAN-IV program, where his legacy of courage and leadership continues to inspire new generations of soldiers.

SN 42987-66589-JA Colonel James Ackerson

James Ackerson was a complex figure within the ranks of the UNSC, known for his strategic maneuvers and contentious relationship with Dr. Catherine Halsey. From a young age, he shared a passion for fantasy literature with his brother Ruwan, which inspired their imaginative quests, particularly for the mythical Key of Osanalan. This childhood fascination would later influence his thinking and decisions as an adult. During his military career, Ackerson trained on Cyrus VII and quickly established himself as a prominent figure within the Office of Naval Intelligence. His political acumen allowed him to secure a position on the UNSC Security Council and influence various high-profile projects, including the development of advanced military technology like the HRUNTING/YGGDRASIL exoskeleton series. His rivalry with Dr. Halsey stemmed from their competing interests and visions for the future of the UNSC's super-soldier programs. Ackerson's ambitions reached a peak when he attempted to undermine the SPARTAN-IIs, with his willingness to go to extreme lengths to assert his dominance and control over military programs. However, his machinations ultimately backfired, and he faced repercussions for his actions. As the Covenant threat escalated, Ackerson's path led him to Mars, where he continued to serve as a combat officer. During the Battle of Mare Erythraeum, he was captured by the Covenant, and in a desperate act to protect his brother and stall the Covenant's advance on Earth, he fabricated a story about the Key of Osanalan. This ploy bought Ruwan time to plan a counterstrike against the Covenant. Tragically, Ackerson's life ended in betrayal, and he was executed by the Jiralhanae after revealing the truth behind his deception. His death marked a pivotal moment, as it not only underscored the risks faced by those involved in the war but also served as a testament to the lengths individuals would go to protect their loved ones. Despite his controversial legacy, Ackerson's name was ultimately honored on the Voi Memorial, a bittersweet recognition of his complex character and the sacrifices made during the tumultuous era of the Human-Covenant War.
